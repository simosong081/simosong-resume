The essay "Accepting and Rejecting Innovation" by Jared Diamond mainly talks about the three main factors, economic advantage, social prestige, and vested interest, which cause people to either accept or reject innovation. I share the same ideas with Diamond, but I have some other opinions on these ideas. Moreover, the first factor is the relative economic advantage when people compare it with the existing technology. Diamond thinks the new technology should offer people enough benefits to make the extra money worthwhile. Some societies do not have the natural resources or materials to support the innovation, so they will have to spend more money on the resource required for it.
